Egusi Soup

Description

Egusi soup is a rich and hearty Nigerian soup made from ground melon seeds, known for its thick texture and deep flavor. Cooked with palm oil, peppers, and assorted meats or fish, it is a staple dish often enjoyed during special occasions and family meals. Egusi soup pairs well with various swallows and is prized for its satisfying taste.

Ingidients

  • Ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • Palm oil
  • Onions
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Tomatoes (optional)
  • Crayfish
  • Locust beans (iru)
  • Assorted meat (beef, goat meat, tripe)
  • Smoked fish or stockfish
  • Meat stock
  • Spinach, bitterleaf, or ugu leaves
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes or powder

Steps

  1. Cook assorted meat and fish until tender and reserve the stock.
  2. Heat palm oil and saut√© onions and peppers.
  3. Add ground egusi and stir to form a thick mixture.
  4. Add meat stock gradually and allow to cook.
  5. Add crayfish, locust beans, and seasoning.
  6. Add cooked meat and fish.
  7. Stir in vegetables and simmer until well combined.
  8. Adjust seasoning and serve with swallow.
