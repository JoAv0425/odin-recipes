Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish known for its rich tomato base, vibrant red color, and bold, smoky flavor. It is typically made by cooking rice in a well-seasoned blend of tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices, allowing the grains to absorb the sauce and develop a deep, savory taste. Often prepared for celebrations and family gatherings, Jollof rice is valued for its simplicity, versatility, and ability to pair well with proteins like chicken, beef, or fish.

Ingidients

  • Long-grain parboiled rice
  • Fresh tomatoes
  • Red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • Scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo)
  • Onions
  • Vegetable oil
  • Tomato paste
  • Chicken or beef stock
  • Curry powder
  • Thyme
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes or powder

Steps

  1. Prepare and blend the tomatoes, peppers, and onions.
  2. Parboil and rinse the rice to remove excess starch.
  3. Cook the tomato mixture with oil, seasoning, and spices to form a rich sauce.
  4. Add stock or water and bring the sauce to a simmer.
  5. Add the rice and allow it to cook while absorbing the sauce.
  6. Adjust seasoning and let the rice steam until fully cooked.
  7. Serve hot, optionally with meat, fish, or vegetables.
